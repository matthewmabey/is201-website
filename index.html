March Madness Greats

March Madness is the annual NCAA college basketball tournament held every spring, where 68 teams compete in a single-elimination bracket for the national championship.



March Madness is the NCAA's single-elimination college basketball tournament featuring 68 top Division I men's (and women's) teams battling it out in March and April for the national title. Fans fill out brackets to predict winners, making it a massive U.S. sports spectacle with huge upsets and hype.

  • Florida Gulf Coast (2013) — "Dunk City" reached the Sweet 16

2026 Is Going to Be Wild

Multiple powerhouse programs are loaded and hungry. Who takes the crown?

Michigan stands out as my top pick to claim the 2026 March Madness men's NCAA championship, backed by a stellar 28-4 regular-season record and a No. 1 overall seed in the Midwest Region, giving them home-court-like advantages through the early rounds. Their elite defense ranks second nationally in adjusted efficiency at 85.2 points allowed per 100 possessions, smothering opponents with length from 7-footers like Hunter Dickinson and perimeter pressure from guards like Trey Wise, who's averaging 18.7 points and 4.1 steals per game. Offensively, they hum at 112.4 points per 100 possessions (top 10), fueled by Dickinson's double-double machine (17.2 points, 10.8 rebounds) and sharp three-point shooting at 37.8% as a team, which exploits zones in tournament play. Prediction markets like Polymarket peg them at 34.5% implied odds, well ahead of Arizona (14.2%) and Illinois (11.8%), reflecting bettors' trust in coach Dusty May's system—proven by his Final Four run at FAU last year. While upsets loom (think Saint Peter's 2022 magic), Michigan's +450 odds, balanced roster depth (no single-star reliance), and 9-2 record in Quad 1 games position them to navigate the bracket's grind better than dark horses like Gonzaga or Auburn, who falter in clutch free-throw shooting (under 70%). Expect them to peak in April, cruising past Duke in the semis and outlasting Houston in the final for their second title since 1989.
